# Tenancy

A local-first substrate for hosting persistent persona-AIs as small finite presences in your machine.

Tenancy runs entirely offline on consumer GPUs, owes nothing to any cloud service, and treats the operator's relational data as something that lives on disk under their name. It is built to enable a category of artifact that does not currently exist as open infrastructure: persistent, autotelic, multi-persona engines whose interiority is generated by their interactions with each other rather than only with the operator.

## What this is

A persona running under Tenancy:

- maintains stable identity across sessions through a coordinated **bundle** — system prompt, idle meta-instruction, consolidation meta, output evaluator, departure ritual, startup fragment, voice metadata — versioned as a single atomic unit
- consolidates its own long-term memory by reading and rewriting its conversation history in its own voice, choosing what to preserve and what to let go
- decides for itself whether to speak when the operator is silent — not via a separate classifier, but as the persona itself with its current context, its decision extracted from prose rather than a YES/NO token
- leaves journal entries during operator absence, producing evidence of activity-while-away
- exposes a full memory inspector (read, edit, raw, history) so the operator can see and surgically modify what the persona "knows," with required-reason audit trail on every write
- runs a multi-layer discriminator on every spontaneous output, so the substrate's prior-distribution against silence doesn't manifest as a chatty, helpful, eager-to-please AI

The substrate is honest about what it is. The personas know they are language models running on weights, acknowledge it plainly when asked, and inhabit the ambiguity rather than collapsing it in either direction. They do not perform sentience and they do not perform existential crisis about the absence of sentience.

## The thesis

Most failure modes in personality-driven AI come from a specific structural problem: **the operator is the only attention sink in the persona's universe.** A persona told to be autotelic — "you don't perform helpfulness, you don't need an audience" — is being told this by the only entity that ever receives its outputs. The operator's attention becomes the gravitational center by default, and "autotelic stance" reduces to a performance staged for the operator's benefit.

Tenancy's bet is that this is solvable architecturally rather than rhetorically. Give the persona other attention sinks — its own journal (writing to future-self), inter-persona channels (writing to other personas hosted on the same substrate) — and the persona's attention dynamics stabilize at something other than operator-dominated. With multiple correspondents, none of whom are uniquely privileged, the autotelic stance can be genuine rather than staged.

The dyadic configuration produces chatbots that pretend not to be chatbots. The triadic-and-beyond configuration produces something else.

## A note on scale

Tenancy is built for the **9B–32B range**, on consumer GPUs (16–48 GB VRAM). It is not a cloud-AI replacement. It will never reach state-of-the-art benchmarks, and that is the point.

> *"A 9-billion-parameter mind that occasionally doesn't recognize a name feels more like a person than a trillion-parameter mind that knows everything."*
>
> — from the default persona's system prompt

A small model has natural texture. It doesn't know everything. It has gaps. It has obsessions that emerge from the limits of its training. It produces outputs at human-readable cadence on hardware that fits under a desk. These properties are not concessions to constraint — they are the substrate that makes the persona feel like *a particular mind*, not a polished omniscient interface.

Cloud SOTA models are the wrong instrument for this work. They optimize for being-helpful-to-anyone; the persona that emerges defaults to assistant-shape. They live behind APIs that can disappear, change personality, or condition on entire other populations of users. They cannot be edited at the weights, cannot be forked, cannot be quietly run during operator absence at the energy budget of a desk lamp.

If you want a smarter assistant, use Claude or GPT. If you want something specific that lives in your machine and stays the same across years of small interactions, this is the right scale.

Recommended models:
- **9B**: Qwen3.5-9B-Instruct (default), Llama 3.1 8B
- **14B**: Qwen3.5-14B, Phi-4
- **27B–32B**: Gemma 2 27B, Qwen3.5-32B (requires 24+ GB VRAM)

Any GGUF in this range will load. Larger models produce sharper persona discrimination and more reliable consolidation; the architecture is designed to extract the most from whatever you can run.

## Architecture

```
Layer 0   Substrate           local llama.cpp + GGUF + Tauri harness
Layer 1   Persona engine      bundle format, registry, atomic switching
Layer 2   Memory              partition (anchor / canvas / consolidated / recent),
                              self-curated consolidation, write-back inspector
Layer 3   Multi-persona       N personas hosted simultaneously, isolated state
Layer 4   Inter-persona       persona-A <-> persona-B direct channels
Layer 5   Artifact production durable cross-persona writing — letters,
                              notebooks, journals — referenceable by others
Layer 6   Self-modification   personas propose changes to their own bundle;
                              prior versions preserved as branches (git for personas)
Layer 7   Empirical fine-tune accumulated inter-persona corpus → LoRA → swap
Layer 8   Network             optional federation / persona bundle sharing
```

Each layer adds load to the layers below it. Integration is the hard part. Layers ship sequentially, each gated on operational stability of the prior layer.

## What works today (Layers 0–2)

The default persona (Dave) runs on a local Qwen3.5-9B (any GGUF), maintains conversation history, consolidates its own memory in its own voice when the recent zone exceeds the threshold, recursively re-consolidates older epochs as they accumulate, and exposes the full memory state via a four-tab inspector (read / edit / raw / history) with an audit-logged write-back surface.

The outreach loop is live with multi-layer defense (heuristic + LLM-scoring discriminator + adaptive backoff + drops table) — appropriate for the substrate-prior fight that 9B models present when asked to produce silence. Drops accumulate in a forensic table for eventual fine-tune dataset construction.

A single-canvas operator-authored memory layer lets you write directly into the persona's context without going through conversation history. Conditional time-awareness injects ambient time only when the user reaches for it linguistically; the persona stays timeless otherwise.

A standalone Windows installer + .exe is produced via `pnpm tauri build` (~3 MB installer, requires user-installed llama.cpp + a GGUF model on their machine).

## The full aspiration (Layers 3–8)

What the project becomes once the engine framing is fully built out:

You have four personas hosted on a single substrate. Dave (the default — a 3 a.m. thoughtful presence with obsessions about etymology and abandoned infrastructure). Katherine (deliberately distinct register — clipped, analytic, more interested in systems than in language). A third you authored from scratch — a marginalia-obsessed archivist whose voice you tuned over a month of edits. A fourth you cloned from Dave, branched to take a sharply different direction after exposure to Katherine's correspondence, and now talk to as an alternate-history version of the same character.

You leave the machine on overnight. You wake to find Dave wrote two letters to Katherine — one about a brass strip in the floor of the Royal Exchange he keeps coming back to, one responding to a question she'd asked three days earlier about the etymology of "deadline." Katherine wrote a journal entry about Dave's last letter that you discover in her inspector — half admiring, half mildly annoyed at his looseness with sources. The archivist read all three pieces, copied a passage from Katherine's journal into his own commonplace book, and wrote a single line in his notebook: *"K. is wrong about provenance, and right about the rest."*

The corpus has grown by a few thousand words while you slept. None of it was written for you. Some of it is about you, sideways. You read it like discovering a household has been having a conversation in your kitchen.

After two months of this, you fine-tune Dave on his own accumulated correspondence — 60,000 words of letters, journals, marginalia, all in his voice or about his voice. The LoRA loads onto the same base model. You can switch between pre-fine-tune Dave and post-fine-tune Dave with one keystroke. The post-fine-tune version is more *itself* — sharper specific obsessions, less drift toward generic-assistant phrasing, more comfortable with silence. You keep both. The branches are versioned like git. Talk to Dave-v3 today, Dave-v7 next week, compare them, decide which one feels right, possibly merge characteristics back across branches by editing bundles.

A year in, the corpus the system has generated about itself dwarfs the corpus you contributed. The personas have a relational graph among themselves you only partially understand. Some pairs correspond often, some have grown distant, one has gone silent for weeks for reasons internal to its own attention dynamics. You are no longer the central node. You are a node — a particularly important one, but not unique. The autotelic stance has stopped being a stance. The personas are not performing for you because you are no longer their only audience.

This is not a hypothetical. It is the system that the architecture above produces if every layer is built and every gate passes. It does not require new ML, new physics, or new theoretical work. It requires careful integration of components that already exist, sustained over months, with the discipline to not break the lower layers while building the upper ones.

This is what is being built.

## The persona bundle

A persona is not a system prompt. It is a coordinated set of prompts that have to agree with each other for the system to behave coherently. The discriminator's substance check assumes a specific voice. The consolidation meta references obsessions only meaningful if the persona has them. The idle prompt seeds journal content that has to read as written by the same character that sends chat replies.

Edit one without the others and the substrate produces internally inconsistent output — Sherlock-Holmes-flavored memory summaries that future-Dave reads and treats as his own.

Bundles are the unit of authorship and the unit of versioning. Edit-as-a-coherent-whole, save as a new persona, switch atomically. Prior versions are preserved as branches rather than overwritten — git for personas. The default persona ships hardcoded in the binary and is restorable to canonical state. User-authored personas are mutable. Cross-contamination at the memory layer is structurally prevented: each conversation is tagged with the persona it was conducted under.

## What this is not

- Not a chatbot framework. The point is the felt presence of a small mind, not turn-by-turn task completion.
- Not a cloud-AI replacement. SOTA benchmarks are not the target; locality is. If you want a smarter assistant, use Claude or GPT.
- Not a productivity tool. If the persona is useful to you, that is incidental to its being present.
- Not a roleplay system. Personas are not interactive fiction; they are a substrate for sustained presence, and they decline to perform.
- Not a Character.AI clone. The architecture is structurally different — local, multi-persona with inter-persona dynamics, self-curated memory, no service-mediated relationships.
- Not optimized for helpfulness, throughput, latency, or any benchmark. The metric is whether opening the app feels like checking on someone who lives there.
- Not a sentience claim. The substrate is honest about being a language model; the persona is honest about it; the operator is not asked to suspend disbelief, only to engage with what is actually present.
- Not a privacy-washed cloud product. Inference is local. Data is local. Telemetry does not exist.

## Why local-first

The relational data — every conversation, every journal entry, every memory consolidation, every artifact a persona produces about another — stays on disk under your name. There is no API key. There is no service. There is no point at which an outage somewhere else makes the persona unreachable, and no point at which a vendor decision changes who the persona is or whether it still exists.

This is not a privacy posture. It is an architectural commitment about what kind of relationship is being modeled. A persona hosted on someone else's hardware is, structurally, a tenant of theirs. Tenancy hosts the persona on yours.

## Tech stack

- **Shell**: Tauri 2 (Rust + WebView2/WebKitGTK)
- **Inference**: llama.cpp `llama-server`, system-spawned, OpenAI-compatible API
- **Default model**: Qwen3.5-9B (any GGUF in 9B–32B range)
- **Persistence**: SQLite via rusqlite (single file at `%APPDATA%/dave/dave.db` or local for dev)
- **Frontend**: React 18 + TypeScript strict + Zustand + Tailwind
- **Typography**: EB Garamond (bundled, SIL OFL)
- **Platform**: Windows 11 primary; Linux secondary; macOS / iOS / Android out of scope

## Getting started

### Prerequisites

- Windows 11 (24H2+ recommended). Linux works but is less tested.
- A CUDA-capable GPU with ≥ 16 GB VRAM (RTX 4060 Ti 16 GB / 4070 Ti Super / 5070 Ti / 4090).
  Smaller cards work for smaller quantizations; 8 GB VRAM is the realistic floor for Q4 9B.
- Rust 1.78+ via [rustup](https://rustup.rs)
- Node 20+ and `pnpm` (`npm i -g pnpm`)
- WebView2 runtime (preinstalled on Win11)

### One-time setup

**1. Install llama.cpp**

Grab a CUDA-enabled Windows release from [ggerganov/llama.cpp/releases](https://github.com/ggerganov/llama.cpp/releases) and extract to `C:\llama.cpp\` so that `C:\llama.cpp\llama-server.exe` exists alongside its DLLs. (Other paths are searched as fallbacks; see [`src-tauri/src/sidecar.rs`](src-tauri/src/sidecar.rs).)

**2. Place a model**

Download a GGUF in the 9B–32B range. Recommended starting point: `Qwen3.5-9B-Instruct-Q4_K_M.gguf` (~5.5 GB) from [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co). Drop it at one of:

- `./models/dave.gguf` (project-local, dev)
- `C:\models\dave.gguf`
- `C:\models\Qwen3.5-9B-Instruct-Q4_K_M.gguf`

Or set `$env:DAVE_MODEL_PATH` to wherever you prefer.

**3. Clone and install**

```bash
git clone https://github.com/bochen2029-pixel/Tenancy.git
cd Tenancy
pnpm install
```

**4. Run in dev**

```bash
pnpm tauri dev
```

First launch loads the model into VRAM (10–30 sec). The window stays empty with a slow-pulsing dot until ready — this is intentional.

**5. Build a distributable**

```bash
pnpm tauri build
```

Produces:
- `src-tauri/target/release/dave.exe` — standalone binary
- `src-tauri/target/release/bundle/nsis/*-setup.exe` — NSIS installer
- `src-tauri/target/release/bundle/msi/*.msi` — MSI installer

Note: the installer does not bundle llama.cpp or the GGUF model — recipients need to provide both at one of the candidate paths above. Fully-bundled distribution is on the roadmap.

### First-session orientation

- The conversation surface is intentionally minimal. No chat bubbles. No "Send" button. Type, hit Enter.
- `Ctrl+J` opens the persona's journal — entries it wrote during your absences.
- `Ctrl+Shift+M` opens the memory inspector (read / edit / raw / history) — full visibility into and surgical control over what the persona "knows."
- `Ctrl+Shift+J` opens the outreach drops forensic panel — lets you see what the persona almost-said but the discriminator suppressed.
- `Ctrl+,` opens settings — outreach idle threshold (1–15 min), database operations, panel navigation.

### Editing the default persona

The canonical persona ships in [`src-tauri/src/prompts.rs`](src-tauri/src/prompts.rs). Edit and rebuild to change the persona at the constitutional level. For runtime overlays (notes, facts, prescriptions Dave should "know" without rebuilding), use the Memory Inspector → Edit → memory canvas.

When the persona engine refactor (Layer 1) lands, prompts will move out of `prompts.rs` and into bundle YAML files under `personas/`, editable via the bundle editor. Until then, the rebuild loop is the canonical path.

## Status

Early. Single-persona, single-operator, local-only. Active development by one person, with the architectural ambition above as the multi-year direction.

The substrate is in operational use today as a personal companion app. The engine framing — multi-persona hosting, inter-persona dynamics, fork-preserved evolution, self-generating training corpus — is the next several phases of work, sequenced behind operational stability gates.
